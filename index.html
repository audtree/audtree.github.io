Welcome to my website! I'm Audrey, a daughter, sister, friend, neighbor, Bay Area community member, and CS PhD at Stanford interested in developing healthcare algorithms that work for all patients. In particular, I investigate how algorithms can promote or challenge health equity, combining statistical algorithmic fairness methods and partnerships with clinicians, patients, and communities. I'm fortunate to be a RAISE fellow and funded by NSF GRFP.

Previously, I graduated from Harvard College studying statistics, computer science, and sociology. During that time, I co-founded and led Harvard's Responsible Computing Collective, Recompute; developed dataset documentation at the Data Nutrition Project; helped teach Harvard's flagship ML course, CS181; and contributed to the EU's Code of Practice on Disinformation at TrustLab, among other shenanigans.

In a past life, I had lots of fun fabricating silicone hinges, raising many yeast children with many mutations, and being green, not mean.
